The Time I Was President For One Day

Today, I was the president of the United States of America for one day. I was thrilled to have this opportunity, and my family and friends congratulated me. When I went to the White House, the security guards checked me and gave me a grand welcome. Then, my assistant took me to my office room and showed me a bunch of files to sign. I spent a lot of time reviewing the files. I signed some files that were for student education, farming, and health care, but I vetoed the bills that are increasing the taxes and lay off the employees by business firms and schools if they don’t have enough funds. My assistant said,” It will be an issue to veto the bills.” I was worried about the public having problems if Congress disagreed on the vetoed bills. I wrote a note to congress and mentioned the importance of the bills and how they helped the people in the country.

After some time, my assistant told me,” Mexico’s president is going to meet and have lunch with you.” I met him with my vice president and team. I am glad that he enjoyed the lunch. After lunch, we were talking about illegal immigrants and border security issues. I assured him I will try to have strong relationships with both countries. But I was worried that Congress would disagree with the illegal immigrants and the border security bills.

A few minutes later, I went to the congress with my vice president and team and told Congress about the illegal immigrants and border security bills. Then my team and I went back to the White House. After we came to the White House, I went to my office to work for some more time. But after some time, my assistant said,” Your family is outside of the white house waiting for you.” I said hello to my family and gave a salute to the security guards. My family and I went on the plane to go home. When we went home, I told all the experiences I had in the white house for one day. They were very happy that I was president of the United States of America for one day.

The End
